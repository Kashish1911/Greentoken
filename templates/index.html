SUSTAINABLE AI TIPS

Reduce Your Cloud Carbon Footprint

🌱
01

Choose Green Regions

GCP Iowa (us-central1) and AWS Oregon (us-west-2) run on near-100% renewable energy. Moving workloads there can cut carbon by up to 88%.

🔋
02

Right-size Instances

Over-provisioned compute is the #1 cloud waste driver. Use auto-scaling and spot/preemptible instances to cut idle compute by 60-70%.

📦
03

Optimize Data Transfer

Egress costs are often hidden. Use CDNs, compress data before transfer, and keep workloads in the same region to minimize both cost and energy.

🤖
04

Use Smaller AI Models

A fine-tuned 7B model can match GPT-4 on domain tasks at 1/14th the energy cost. Choose the smallest model that meets your accuracy requirement.

⏱
05

Batch AI Requests

Batching 100 requests together uses ~3× less energy per request than sending them individually due to GPU utilization and overhead amortization.

🗜
06

Compress & Cache

Cache AI responses for repeated queries. Enable storage compression (up to 4:1 ratio for logs). Use lifecycle policies to auto-archive cold data to cheaper, greener tiers.

🎯 Token Optimization Strategies
Prompt Compression
Remove filler words, use abbreviations in system prompts. Average 30-40% token reduction possible without quality loss.
~35% fewer tokens
Response Caching
Cache identical or near-identical queries. In enterprise settings, 15-40% of queries are repetitive and can be served from cache.
~25% cost reduction
Model Cascading
Route simple queries to small models (7B), complex ones to large models. 70-80% of queries are typically simple.
~60% energy saving
Streaming & Early Stop
Use max_tokens limits. Set temperature=0 for deterministic tasks. Avoid regenerating when partial results satisfy the need.
~20% token saving
